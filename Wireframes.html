Collaborate for the greater good

Discarding blows. Recycling rocks. Upcycling digital resources to create products of a better quality — and higher value — than the original is simply the bomb. An explosion of everything awesome for the greater good of any Magnet 360 project.

But please, just experience what that means. Learn to upcycle your work simply because it's hard work. M360 Upcycler just helps make it easier, simpler, and faster to leverage assets routinely and in perpetuity during any project kick-off.

More than this, it's shareable, collaborative, and community moderated by default, for predictably establishing industry standards and constantly evolving best practices. And ultimately, it nets more sustainable and viable returns on investment than always having to start projects from scratch.

Work hard — and smart — for the greater good, with M360 Upcycler.

Hit the ground running

Once everything is local, or as soon as you become an official collaborator, you can Clone, Create, and Commit your upcycle right away, as in rapidly build your responsive presentation for Github distribution.

For best practice, make upcycling part of your regular routine during Sprint Retros then Chatter about it — so that everyone can immediately reap its rewards in the very same way that you yourself can take advantage of everybody else's. All you really need to get started is a working knowledge of HTML, CSS, Javascript, and Git/hub; tradecraft that you need to constantly hone anyway.

And for the continuous improvement of everyone's upcycles, you can contribute further by creating an issue on Github "to track todos, bugs, feature requests, and more..." just as you would on any open source community project. That's the entire workflow at high level, so by all means, simply follow these steps to hit the ground running:

Clone
Locally, with all your repositories, preferably in Egnyte, which is actually in the Cloud
CRUD
Dupe this page then Create, Retrieve, Update, or Delete your upcycles, preferably with Sublime Text 3, so they could be easily used and reused in Salesforce through MavensMate
Commit
Sync, Commit, and Resync on Github, then with Raw Git, publish and view your upcycle online like any other site
Chatter
Share, promote, and discuss your upcycle on Salesforce Chatter, preferably by hash-tagging its dedicated topic (#M360Upcycler)
Contribute
Create an Issue on Github to track todos, bugs, feature requests, and more. Even better, help others fix them

Upcycle like a hipster Rockstar

So you already know that M360 Upcycler is built with Semantic UI — "a development framework that helps create beautiful, responsive layouts using human-friendly HTML."

You have long discovered that it "treats words and classes as exchangeable concepts," uses simple Javascript phrases "called behaviors that trigger functionality," and lets you "track down bottlenecks without digging through stack traces" as demonstrated by this short clip for Simplified Debugging

Since all that just about makes you yawn, now you really want to learn:

How to use and create custom themes for your UI components How to compile themes and get started with command-line tools How to customize Semantic UI and "design for 'the long' now, right now"

Well, then, have at it. Play with Semantic UI like a rockstar. But please share the limelight backstage. Teach us the way to the hall of fame in here, our M360 Upcycler. We need all the help we can get, especially from rockstars like you, so go ahead. Play your heart out.
